What You Can Do on an F1 Visa

  • Live in the U.S. while pursuing a full-time academic program
  • Work on-campus up to 20 hours per week during academic terms
  • Work full-time during official school breaks and summer vacation
  • Participate in Curricular Practical Training (CPT) related to your field of study
  • Apply for Optional Practical Training (OPT) after completing your studies
  • Transfer to another SEVP-approved school
  • Travel in and out of the U.S. with proper documentation
  • Obtain a driver's license in your state of residence (varies by state)

F1 Visa Restrictions

  • Cannot work off-campus without authorization
  • Cannot participate in unauthorized employment
  • Must maintain a full course of study each academic term
  • Cannot receive federal public assistance
  • Must maintain a valid passport from your home country
  • Must depart the U.S. within 60 days of program completion (unless transferring or changing status)
  • Cannot study at public elementary schools or adult education programs

Employment Information

As an F1 student, you are limited in the types of employment you may engage in:

On-Campus Employment

You may work up to 20 hours per week while school is in session and full-time during breaks.

Curricular Practical Training (CPT)

CPT must be an integral part of your established curriculum and directly related to your major field of study.

Optional Practical Training (OPT)

You may apply for up to 12 months of OPT authorization upon completion of your academic program.

Travel Information

When traveling outside the U.S., you will need the following documents to re-enter:

  • A valid passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond your date of entry)
  • A valid F1 visa stamp (unless traveling to Canada, Mexico, or adjacent islands for less than 30 days)
  • A valid Form I-20 with a travel endorsement from your DSO (less than 1 year old)
  • Financial evidence showing proof of necessary funds
  • Your most recent transcript and proof of enrollment
If your visa has expired, you will need to renew it at a U.S. embassy or consulate before returning to the U.S.

Maintaining Your Status

To maintain your F1 status, you must:

  • Attend the school you are authorized to attend
  • Maintain a full course of study each academic term
  • Make normal progress toward completing your course of study
  • Keep your passport valid at all times
  • Follow proper procedures for transferring, extending, or changing your education level
  • Limit on-campus employment to 20 hours per week while school is in session
  • Update your residential address within 10 days of moving

Social Security Number

If you plan to work in the U.S., you will need a Social Security Number (SSN). You can apply for an SSN at a Social Security Administration office after securing employment.
