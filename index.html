What You Can Do on a Student Visa

  • Study at the University of Glasgow for your approved course
  • Work up to 20 hours per week during term time
  • Work full-time during official university holidays
  • Take unpaid work experience as part of your course
  • Do voluntary work for a registered charity
  • Travel in and out of the UK with proper documentation
  • Switch to a different course at the same or higher level
  • Apply for a Graduate visa after completing your studies
  • Open a UK bank account with your university support
  • Register with the NHS for healthcare services

Student Visa Restrictions

  • Cannot work more than 20 hours per week during term time
  • Cannot be self-employed or start a business
  • Cannot work as a professional sportsperson or entertainer
  • Cannot claim public funds or benefits
  • Cannot study at a different institution without permission
  • Must maintain attendance and academic progress
  • Cannot marry or enter civil partnership as a visitor
  • Must leave the UK when your visa expires (unless extending or switching)

Employment Information

As a Student visa holder, you have limited work rights in the UK:

Part-time Work

You may work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during official university holidays.

Work Placement

Work placements as part of your course are allowed and don't count towards your 20-hour limit if they're less than 50% of your total study time.

Graduate Route

After completing your degree, you may be eligible for a Graduate visa allowing you to work in the UK for 2 years (3 years for PhD graduates).

Travel Information

When traveling outside the UK, you will need the following documents to re-enter:

  • A valid passport (valid for the duration of your stay)
  • Your valid BRP card (Biometric Residence Permit)
  • Current CAS statement or university enrollment letter
  • Evidence of financial support if requested
  • Academic transcripts showing your progress
  • Accommodation details in the UK
If your BRP card is lost, stolen, or damaged while abroad, contact the UK embassy or consulate immediately.

Maintaining Your Status

To maintain your Student visa status, you must:

  • Study at the University of Glasgow as authorized
  • Attend classes regularly and make satisfactory academic progress
  • Keep your passport and BRP card valid at all times
  • Inform the university of any changes to your personal details
  • Not exceed your work hour limitations
  • Report to police if required (certain nationalities)
  • Extend your visa before it expires if continuing studies
  • Maintain adequate funds for your studies and living costs

National Insurance Number

If you plan to work in the UK, you will need a National Insurance Number (NINo). You can apply online or by phone once you start working.

NHS Healthcare

Register with a local GP practice near your university accommodation. You may be entitled to free NHS healthcare as a student visa holder.
